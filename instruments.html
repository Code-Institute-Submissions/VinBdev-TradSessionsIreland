    Number 6: The Mandolin

    The Mandolin is an 8 stringed instrument that combines four sets of two closely paired strings. Together these strings give a sharper plucky sound when played as opposed to a guitar which would have more long lasting notes. The mandolin can someitmes be confused with the banjo for this reason however it is not a traditional Irish musical instrument as it originates from Italy in the 1800's.You can read more about the mandolin.

    Number 7: The Bodhrán

    The Bodhrán is a percussion instrument that although may seem underwelming in comparison to a full drum set, this instrument can carry many songs on its own and is incredibly easy to use. This is often made of goat skin and wood. In Ireland, most school kids will make a bodhrán at least once throughout their time in school. This is often done to encourage the crafts of Irish culture but also to encourage the creative younger generations to play the instrument.

    Weeks:1-2
    --------------Introductory songs
    Month: 1-3
    --------Intermediate Songs
    Month: 3-6
    -----Upper Intermediate Songs
    Month: 12+
    ---Advanced Songs

    Number 8: Concertina

    The concertina is oftern confused witht the accordion above and is also a commonly used instrument for our trad sessions. The instrument is easier to use than an accordion however this still requires an abundance of skill and practice to play well. Both the accordion and concertina are free-reed musical instrumetns that consist of contracting air between bellows while using vaarious buttons to release a wailing sound that can carry across a room and add a sense of tradition to any session.

    Number 9: Harmonica

    The Harmonica is a mouth organ instrument that although may seem small, it is also a powerful and very commonly used instrument within musical acts all around the world. It is the only instrument on this list that can fit in your pocket and therefore it is a great skill for any musician to have. This instrument normally comes in two sizes which allow a 10/ 16 hole harmonicas. This too is commonly seen in various types jazz, blues, classical, rock and American folk music.
